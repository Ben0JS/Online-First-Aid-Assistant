Bleeding

Apply firm pressure with a clean cloth, keep the injured part elevated, and seek medical help.

Burns

Cool the burn under running water for 10 minutes, cover with sterile gauze, avoid ice or butter.

Read More

Choking

Encourage coughing, perform abdominal thrusts (Heimlich maneuver) if necessary.

Read More

Fractures

Immobilize the injured area, apply a splint, and seek medical help immediately.

Read More

Sprains

Rest the injured area, apply ice, compress with a bandage, and elevate the limb.

Read More

Heatstroke

Move to a cool place, hydrate, and apply cool cloths. Seek urgent medical help.

Read More

Hypothermia

Move to warmth, remove wet clothing, wrap in blankets, and seek medical attention.

Read More

Heart Attack

Call emergency services, keep the person calm, and give aspirin if advised.

Read More

Stroke

Remember FAST: Face drooping, Arm weakness, Speech issues, Time to call help.

Read More

Asthma Attack

Help use inhaler, keep the person upright, and call for emergency help if severe.

Read More

Seizures

Clear the area, protect the head, do not restrain, and roll on the side after seizure ends.

Read More

Shock

Lay the person down, keep them warm, elevate legs if possible, and seek medical care.

Read More

Allergic Reaction

Identify allergen, give antihistamine or epinephrine if prescribed, and call emergency help.

Read More

Poisoning

Do not induce vomiting, call poison control immediately, and provide details of the substance.

Read More

Nosebleeds

Sit upright, lean forward, pinch the nostrils, and apply a cold compress.

Read More

Drowning

Remove the person from water, call for help, start CPR if necessary.

Read More

Electric Shock

Turn off power source, do not touch the person until safe, check breathing, and call help.

Read More

Eye Injury

Rinse with clean water for 15 minutes, do not rub, and seek medical attention.
